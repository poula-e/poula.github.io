Ancient Egyptian writing



The emergence of ancient Egyptian writing

Writing had a major role in the development of Egyptian civilization Hieroglyphic writing appeared first on the boards of the Edwaz The emergence of writing in Egypt coincided with the period when the language of speech with Semitic elements strengthened the language of speech with protective elements because the method of writing and notation was originally to record the Semitic way of pronunciation and speech

Uses of writing in ancient Egypt

Writing was used in all affairs of life as it was used in religious writings on the walls of temples and pyramids, and was used in public affairs and royal orders, and was used in education and other life affairs and also used in literature and the ancient Egyptians excelled in it and their prowess appeared in it, which is one of the most important uses of writing in ancient Egypt.


Ancient Egyptian Writing Instruments

In ancient Egyptian civilization, writers used a variety of tools for writing. Let's review these tools:

  • Linen pens: They were made of reeds and used for writing
  • Inks: They were used for writing on papers, porcelain and wood. Writers use inks to record official and administrative documents of the state.
  • Papyrus: It was made from the stems of the papyrus plant, these leaves were soft and beautiful, and were used to write on them in black and red ink.
  • Porcelain and wood pieces: If papyrus is not available, porcelain or wood pieces could have been used for writing

Literature in Ancient Egypt

  • Literature for the Egyptians was one of the most important colors of intellectual life and was used by the famous Egyptian writings, and its reign dates back to the time of the builders of the pyramids, but the most wonderful thing that remained of it is what was written in the era of the Middle Kingdom, and its types are as follows:
  1. Religious literature: It is like the texts of the pyramids and the book of the dead and what was inscribed on the walls of worships and tombs, as well as songs and hymns, such as what Akhenaten left us in describing and reverence for his gods, and the Egyptians left a lot of that literature.
  2. Fictional literature: It included fictional or real stories, for example, the story of 'the eloquent farmer'
  3. Refinement literature: It is a teachings and commandments recommended by good advice and may be said by Pharaoh or teachers or parents or wise men and women and the most famous of those teachings is what was left by Ptah Hotep in the era of the Old Kingdom, and Ani in the era of the New Kingdom
  4. Literature of praise: One of the most famous excelled in it was Senusret III and was often intended to glorify Pharaoh and his deeds and heroism.



Writing in ancient Egypt is divided into several stages due to the different times and circumstances, which are as follows:

I.Hieroglyphs:

4000 BCE:3500 BCE

It prevailed in the Pharaonic times from its beginning in the ancient era until the end of the Pharaonic state after the New Kingdom and then began to become extinct, it was usually written from right to left or from left to right, horizontal or vertical, which are references that include what is in nature from man, animal, plant, water, sun and other natural phenomena.«each sign was written with great attention to the details of the drawing, for example, the bird does not only show it with its side lines, but also with its various internal features, with wings, eyes and claws... etc.

II.Hieratic:

3000 BCE:Not Mentioned

Due to the difficulty of hieroglyphs and the difficulty of learning and using them in public affairs, hieratic writing was invented and is derived from hieroglyphs with some simplification,It was also called the priestly calligraphy, which means the line of the clergy, because the priests and clergy were the ones who used this writing a lot in all their works, and it was written on ceramics and wood, and most of the literary writings of the Egyptians were recorded in hieratic.

III.Demoticism:

8th century BC:5th century AD

It prevailed after the end of the modern state in the era of the last decay because of its ease on hieroglyphs and hieratics, and it was also called colloquially because of its spread among all the people and it was their vernacular language, but it was written.

IIII.Coptic:

Beginning of the 3rd century:The last stage in the development of writing and language

It is an ancient Egyptian written in Greek letters, and the Christian clergy recorded all their writings and religious texts, and that language prevailed along with many because of the diversity of those who lived in Egypt in that period, from the pharaohs, then the Persians, the Ptolems, the Greeks, the Greeks, the Jews and even the Romans, so there were many languages and writings, so certain writing prevailed in a certain era and also for each writing field this difference and diversity was clearly shown in the Roman era, and then the Islamic conquest of Egypt took place, and thus other languages began to decline until Arabic dominated, but it is not considered one of the writings of ancient Egypt because it did not prevail until relatively advanced times, contrary to what is rumored.
